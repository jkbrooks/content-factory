# Steel-Manning the Non-Human Intelligence Evidence

Most treatments of UAP/NHI evidence fail at a basic epistemic level: they either argue too hard for the phenomenon (credulity) or dismiss it too quickly (motivated skepticism). A rigorous analyst should be able to hold the following simultaneously:

1. The evidence base is genuinely anomalous — there are documented cases where sensor data, eyewitness accounts, and physical effects cannot be explained by conventional aircraft, atmospheric phenomena, or known sensor artifacts.

2. Anomalous ≠ extraterrestrial. The inference from "we can't explain this" to "it must be non-human intelligence" involves several unjustified logical leaps.

3. The social and institutional dynamics around UAP reporting create severe selection effects. Credible witnesses (military, commercial pilots) face career disincentives for reporting. This means the visible evidence is almost certainly a small subset of actual events.

4. The strongest evidence is not individual sighting reports but the pattern: multi-sensor confirmation, correlated radar tracks, simultaneous observation by independent witnesses, and physical effects on electronics and observers.

A probabilistic framework helps. If you assign even a 5% prior to "at least some UAP represent genuine non-human technology or intelligence," and then update on the best documented cases (Nimitz, Roosevelt, Gimbal), the posterior moves meaningfully. The question is not "is this certain?" but "does this evidence update us above or below some threshold?"

The analytically honest position: the phenomenon is real in the sense that something is occurring that we can't explain with public information. Whether that something involves intelligence is a separate, harder question.
